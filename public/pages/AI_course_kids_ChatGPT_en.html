🤖 1 · What is an LLM?

An LLM is a language-pattern machine. It builds answers by predicting likely next pieces of text.

LLM means Large Language Model. It is trained on many examples of language and sometimes other data, depending on the model. It learns patterns: which words often appear together, how explanations are built, and what kind of answer usually fits a question.

What is a token?

A token is a small piece of text. It can be a whole word, part of a word, a number, or punctuation.

Useful for

Explaining, summarising, rewriting, translating, brainstorming, practising, and helping with code.

⚠️

Needs checking for

Exact facts, recent events, maths, quotes, sources, medical or legal advice, and anything important.

Key word: hallucination. This means the AI gives a wrong answer that sounds confident. It happens because the model generates likely text; it does not automatically prove that the text is true.

🔒 4 · Privacy + age rules

A 12-year-old should use AI only with parent or teacher permission.

Do not paste

Passwords, addresses, phone numbers, private photos, private school/work documents, unpublished data, medical details, or anything that could embarrass someone.

Safer to use

Made-up examples, public facts, short text you are allowed to share, and questions without personal details.

!

Before sending, ask: “Would it be okay if this became public or was reviewed by a company?” If the answer is no, do not paste it.

Age reality check

Some AI services require users to be at least 13 with parent permission. Some require 18+. Some school or family accounts have special controls. Always check the rules for the exact tool you use.

✍️ 5 · Prompting: how to ask well

A good prompt gives the AI a clear mission.

Weak prompt:
Explain AI.

Better prompt:
Explain what an AI chatbot is for a 12-year-old. Use 5 short bullet points, one gaming example, and one thing I should double-check.

Prompt recipe

Goal: What should it make?
Audience: Who is it for?
Context: What does it need to know?
Format: Bullets, table, quiz, story?
Checks: Ask what might be wrong.

Useful follow-ups

“Make it shorter.”
“Give an example.”
“What could be wrong?”
“Use simpler words.”
“Show two versions.”

🧠 7 · Context window and context rot

A chatbot can only work with the information currently available to it.

The context window is like the AI’s desk. It may include your prompt, recent chat history, uploaded text, and instructions. If the chat becomes crowded, old details can be ignored, mixed up, or contradicted.

Signs of context rot

  • The AI forgets the goal.
  • It mixes old and new instructions.
  • It repeats a mistake.
  • It answers a question you did not ask.

Fix it

  • Start a new chat.
  • Paste a short summary.
  • List only the important facts.
  • Give one clear next task.
Restart - open new chat:

Goal: [goal]. Important facts: [facts]. Decisions so far: [decisions]. Next task: [task]. Please continue with your [analysis/writing/explanation]

✅ 8 · What to trust, what to verify

Do not trust all AI answers equally.

LOW RISK
Rewriting, brainstorming, style ideas
CHECK IT
Science, code, units, school answers
VERIFY ALWAYS
Numbers, dates, quotes, sources, private data
Mini quiz: should you trust it?

1. AI rewrites your paragraph more clearly. Usually okay, but read it once.

2. AI gives a quote from a book. Check it. Quotes can be invented.

3. AI converts 37 °C to Kelvin. Use the formula or a calculator. 37 °C = 310.15 K.

4. AI explains photosynthesis simply. Good starting point, but compare with class material.

🧪 One-page cheat sheet

AI can help me…

Understand, draft, rewrite, organise, brainstorm, practise, compare, and make examples.

I must check…

Facts, numbers, dates, sources, personal data, and anything that will be graded, published, or shared.

Copy-and-use prompt:

You are a friendly teacher. Explain [TOPIC] for a 12-year-old. Use short sentences, one example, and a tiny quiz. Also tell me what I should double-check.
